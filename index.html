Orton-Gillingham

The Orton-Gillingham approach is specifically designed for working with students with dyslexia, but it can also help all students with reading difficulties. It uses a multisensory approach to teach phonics specifics, syllable types, and spelling patterns. All skills are taught auditorily, visually, and physically. It is a cumulative approach that reinforces learned skills each lesson. (include binder doc on this page)

A typical lesson consists of an audio review of sounds, visual review of sounds, blending practice, reading practice, syllabication, new concept instruction, reinforcement of new concept thought auditory, visual, and physical practice, and spelling with the new and old concepts. Each session ends with reading practice.
